IronPigs End Alford's Streak
<Buffalo:team#176> center fielder <Anthony Alford:player#32849> went 0-for-4 today in <Buffalo:team#176>'s 6-2 triumph over the <Lehigh Valley IronPigs:team#158>, snapping his 27-game hitting streak.

Alford spoke to BNN after the game and said he "knew the hitting streak would end sooner or later."

"I just would have preferred later," he said with a laugh. "It was fun though."

When Alford made an out in his last chance at the plate, his teammates congratulated him in the dugout. In the clubhouse after the game, he seemed unbothered by the streak ending.

"There's nothing scientific about it, that's for sure," IronPigs manager <Gary Jones:coach#2247> said of Alford's streak. "He had a nice little run. Let him start another one when we finish the series here."

This season <Alford:player#32849> is batting .277 with 66 hits, 7 home runs, 36 RBIs, 19 walks, .332 OBP and has scored 32 runs. He has played in 62 games.
